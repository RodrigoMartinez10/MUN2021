European Council

Committee Description

The European Council is one of the seven institutions of the European Union, and it is in charge of defining political directions and priorities for the agenda of the EU. It was officially established in the Treaty of Lisbon in 2009. It is formed by the heads of state of each government member of the European Union.

The meetings of this committee are held at least four times a year in the Europa building (sometimes referred to as the egg) in Brussels, Belgium. Although this commission represents the heads of state of the governments, it has no legislative power. Its purpose is mostly to provide strategies acting as a unified government. Because of the nature of its power and influence, it is considered to be the main pillar of European integration.
